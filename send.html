Suggested subject line

Paste into a brand-new email, do not hit "Forward." Forwarding adds "Fwd:" and quoted clutter. Open a fresh compose window, paste, add the recipients, and send. It'll look like you wrote it yourself.

Step 2 — Paste & send

After clicking Copy newsletter above, follow the steps for whichever email you use. Everyone gets the same result.

Gmail Web

  1. Click Compose.
  2. Click inside the message body.
  3. Paste — ⌘ V (Mac) or Ctrl V (Windows).
  4. Add your subject and recipients, then hit Send.

Apple Mail Looks best

  1. Click the pencil (New Message).
  2. Click in the body area.
  3. Paste — ⌘ V.
  4. Add your subject and recipients, then hit Send.

A couple of notes

On your phone or in Outlook? The paste still works the same way: new message, tap in the body, paste. If any client strips the layout, share this link instead so folks can view the full version in a browser: mosejames.github.io/rcap-exp-2026.

Images not showing after paste? Some inboxes hide remote images until you click "Display images" or "Load images." That's on the reader's side, not the copy. The photos are hosted online, so once images are allowed, they appear.
