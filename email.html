Dear Ron Clark Academy Families,

Today was our first summer EXP, and RCAP showed up and showed out.

Parents spent the day doing a hundred things, big and small, that helped make the day a success: greeting guests, directing traffic, guiding educators, working registration, serving meals, flipping rooms, taking photos, and answering questions.

1,000+
Educators welcomed, from across the country & the world
01  Arrival & parking

The first thing visitors experience is us.

Some educators arrive on charter buses. Others drive themselves. Some take an Uber. Every now and then, someone even pulls up in a driverless car. However they arrive, we help them feel welcome from the moment they step onto campus.

Before the first session begins, the parking lot is already a team sport.

“
One of the first things visitors experience when they arrive at RCA is us.
02  Registration

A thousand educators. One smooth check-in.

With more than 1,000 educators on campus, registration is a major operation. Volunteers hand out name badges, schedules, notebooks, and materials, and make sure everyone gets exactly where they need to go.

Badges, schedules, notebooks, a warm hello, handed over hundreds of times before lunch.

03  Keeping everyone moving

It looks effortless. It's teamwork.

Under the steady command of Mr. Walker, RCA's unofficial Director of Educator Movement, parents help guide group after group through the building. Keeping a thousand educators flowing smoothly from room to room takes real coordination and teamwork.

Follow the leader: a group of educators move to their next session.

04  Flipping rooms & serving meals

Tables appear, disappear, and reappear.

If you've ever watched an EXP day unfold, you've seen rooms transform again and again. Tables move. Meals are served. Sessions begin. It's a lot of work, and it only happens because parents jump in wherever they're needed.

Meal support runs on volunteers who show up early and smile straight through the rush.

“
It only happens because parents jump in wherever they're needed.
Behind every flipped room
05  The people you meet

The unexpected benefit of showing up.

You'll meet educators from all over the world. You'll run into old friends. You'll hear incredible stories. And every now and then, you'll catch a glimpse of an RCA lesson in action, and walk away with an even deeper appreciation for what happens inside these walls every day.

Old friends, new friends, and the kind of laugh you only get when you're in it together.

06  The real stars

Of course, the day belonged to our students.

Polos. Khakis. Smiles. Leadership.

Our students represented RCA beautifully and reminded us why educators travel thousands of miles to see what makes this place so special.

Freshly washed and ironed polos. Khakis. And smiles for days.

From all of us at RCAP
Thank you.

To every parent, student, staff member, alumni volunteer, and family member who helped make today a success.
